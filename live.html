Phase 1

Open

Dancing Mad (Ultimate) recommends a 0-second burst (open with burst right at the pull).
Because Phase 2's enrage is at 6:20, continuing the 0-second burst from Phase 1 nets you roughly 5–6 extra seconds of burst damage overall.
These 5–6 seconds are huge — they directly determine whether you can fit in one extra use of a powerful ability.

💡 In plain terms

Why you open with everything at the pull: Phase 2 ends in a hard enrage at 6:20, which is tight. If the party fires its raid buffs and burst the instant the boss is targetable (a “0-second” opener) and keeps every 2-minute window aligned, you bank ~5–6 seconds of extra uptime by the end of P2 — often exactly enough for one more big oGCD across the group. Don’t hold cooldowns waiting for a “safe” moment.

Ruinga

Tankbuster (90° cone)
The 1st hits the player 1st in enmity; the 2nd hits the player 2nd in enmity.

💡 In plain terms

What it is (Revolting Ruin III): a heavy 90° frontal cone that fires twice — once on #1 enmity, once on #2. The clean way to handle it is a tank swap so both hits land on the main tank (MT eats hit 1, then re-provokes/invulns for hit 2), point the cone away from the party, and heal it. Heads-up: damage-downs in this fight are a brutal 90% for 3 minutes, so absolutely never clip a non-tank into the cleave.

Statue of the Gods (1st)

A tether attaches to a tank/healer or a DPS, and then you're knocked back from the statue.

💡 In plain terms

Graven Image (1st): a tether links the statue to one whole role group — it goes to all tanks/healers or all DPS — and after a short delay it knocks that group back from the statue. Standard setup splits the arena DPS East / Supports West; ride the knockback to your final spot rather than fighting it.

① Missing — priority order + positions (Pylene)

After Missing finishes casting, debuffs are applied: 2 stacks, 3 circle AoEs, and 3 cone AoEs.
The stack always goes to 1 tank/healer and 1 DPS.
The circle AoEs are grouped onto either the tanks/healers or the DPS, and the cones likewise.
So tanks/healers get either ⟨stack・circle・circle・circle⟩ or ⟨stack・cone・cone・cone⟩, and DPS likewise.
Form pairs in advance (MT&H1 / ST&H2 / D1&D3 / D2&D4); the pair that got the stack soaks the 1st of the 8 towers that appear (each tower shifts 45° clockwise or counterclockwise).
Then resolve the towers by the set rule, and repeat the same process with the newly applied markers — that's the mechanic.
The rules below are a method that makes things easier for H1・H2・D3・D4.

💡 In plain terms

Missing — the big one: when Missing finishes, everyone gets a shape over their head plus 4 stacks of a debuff. The shapes resolve like this: Stack = 3-person shared hit, Circle = spread AoE on you, Cone = 45° cone onto the nearest player. The only way to remove a debuff stack is to soak a tower — and every time you soak one, your shape goes off, so you must be positioned correctly each time. Eight towers spawn in total, each rotated 45° from the last.

This phase is especially demanding for healers' healing, so the rules are designed to keep the resolution load as low as possible.
Also, while this mechanic's flow is simple, the hardest part is unified positioning (detailed positions shown in images at the end).

💡 In plain terms

Why the fuss: this is the hardest healing stretch in P2 because of the constant shape damage on top of towers. The rules below just trade brain-effort for a calmer, lower-damage resolution — the mechanic itself is simple, the hard part is everyone standing in exactly the same spot.
